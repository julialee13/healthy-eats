Why Live Here?

While convenience is often the number one reason for living on campus, you’ll find we offer our students much more than just a place to study and sleep. University Housing provides opportunities for student growth and development through educational, cultural, recreational, and social programs presented in our living & learning communities.

Housing Options

Patton Hall

The suites feature a lockable built-in closet for each resident. Each room includes two windows with shades, and both bedrooms are connected by a full bathroom.

Explore »

University Commons

The Commons, a complex of four apartment buildings ranging from 8 to 15 stories houses approximately 2,000 Georgia State University students, primarily freshmen.

Explore »

The Lofts

The Lofts are fully-furnished loft style apartments that blend urban style with modern-day conveniences. There are studios, one, two, three, and four bedroom apartments.

Explore »

Greek Housing

Greek organizations may uniquely decorate their town homes so members have a sense of living in a “traditional” house, with all of the convenience of living on campus.

Explore »

Piedmont North

Offers a traditional residence hall lifestyle with all the amenities one would expect from a premier housing program.

Explore »

Price Center

Housing that is most central on campus for those that don't like to travel far!
